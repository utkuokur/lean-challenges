\section*{The Sphere Packing Problem}
\refstepcounter{section}

\begin{definition} 
Fix a dimension $r \in \N$. Consider the vector space $\R^r$, equipped with Euclidean distance and Lebesgue measure $\mathrm{Vol}(\cdot)$. For $x \in \R^r$ and $\varepsilon >0$, let $B_r\ev{x,\varepsilon}$ denote the open ball in $\R^r$, with center $x$ and radius $\varepsilon$. 
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \emph{sphere packing} of $\R^r$ is a set $C$ of points in $\R^r$ that have pairwise distance at least two. 
\item The \emph{upper density} of a sphere packing $C$ is
$$
\bar\delta_r\ev{C} := \limsup_{R \to \infty} \frac{\mathrm{Vol}\!\ew{\textstyle\bigcup_{x \in C} B\ev{x,1} \cap B\ev{0,R}}}{\mathrm{Vol}\!\ew{B\ev{0,R}}}.
$$
The \emph{sphere-packing constant} is $\Delta_r := \sup_C \bar\delta_r\ev{C}$ where the supremum ranges over all sphere packings $C$. 

\item A \textit{lattice packing} of $\R^r$ is a sphere packing $C$ which is also a lattice in $\R^r$, i.e., $C = \{ \sum_{i=1}^{r} a_i v_i : a_1,\ldots, a_r \in \Z \} $ for some basis $\{ v_1, \dots, v_r \}$ of $\R^r$. The \emph{lattice-packing constant} is $\Delta_r^{L} := \sup_C \bar\delta_r\ev{C}$ where the supremum ranges over all lattice packings of $\R^r$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The sphere packing problem is to determine $\Delta_r$. It is not even known whether densest sphere packings always need to be attained by lattices:

\begin{openquestion}
Given $r\geq 1$, is it true that $\Delta_r = \Delta_r^{L}$?
\end{openquestion}

In the literature, the equation $\Delta_r = \Delta_r^{L}$ is only shown for $r \in \{1,2,3,8,24\}$. The case $r=3$ is known as the Kepler conjecture, proven by  Hales \cite{hales2005kepler}. The cases $r = 8$ and $r = 24$ were settled by Viazovska \cite{viazovska2017eight} and by Cohn, Kumar, Miller, Radchenko and Viazovska \cite{cohn2017twentyfour}, respectively.
