\section*{Challenge 7: The Sphere Packing Problem}
\refstepcounter{section}


For a dimension $d\in\N$, a \emph{unit packing} of $\R^d$ is a set $C\subseteq\R^d$ of centres with $\lVert x-y\rVert\geq 2$ for all distinct $x,y\in C$, so that the open unit balls around the centres are pairwise disjoint. Its \emph{upper density} is
$$
\bar\delta\ev{C}=\limsup_{R\to\infty}\frac{\mathrm{vol}\!\ew{\textstyle\bigcup_{x\in C}B\ev{x,1}\cap B\ev{0,R}}}{\mathrm{vol}\!\ev{B\ev{0,R}}},
$$
and the \emph{sphere-packing constant} $\Delta_d=\sup_C\bar\delta\ev{C}$ is the supremum over all unit packings; $\Delta_d^{L}$ denotes the supremum over packings whose centres form a lattice.

\begin{openproblem}[The sphere-packing problem]
Determine the sphere-packing constant $\Delta_d$ for every dimension $d$. The value is known only for $d\in\{1,2,3,8,24\}$: the case $d=3$ is the Kepler conjecture \cite{hales2005kepler}, and $d=8$, $d=24$ were settled by Viazovska \cite{viazovska2017eight} and by Cohn, Kumar, Miller, Radchenko and Viazovska \cite{cohn2017twentyfour}. The largest solved case is $d=24$, and in each the optimum is attained by a lattice ($\Z$, $A_2$, the FCC lattice, $E_8$, and the Leech lattice).
\end{openproblem}

The open problem asks whether lattices are always optimal: is $\Delta_d = \Delta_d^{L}$ for every $d$?
