Challenge 7: Rota’s Basis Conjecture

The following conjecture in its original form [2, Conjecture 4] is stated in terms of matroids, but even in the special case where the matroid is a vector matroid – that is, the bases come from a finite-dimensional vector space – the conjecture is widely open.

Conjecture 1.1 (Rota’s basis conjecture).

Let M be a finite matroid of rank n with a family (B1,..,Bn) of disjoint bases. There is a family of disjoint bases (C1,..Cn) of M so that Bi∩Cj is a singleton for all i,j∈[n].

Here we are interested in the following scaled weakening of the original conjecture.

Conjecture 1.2 (Scaled weakening of Rota’s basis conjecture).

Let r∈ℕ. There is n0∈ℕ so that for all n≥n0 the following holds: for every finite matroid M of rank n with a family (B1,..,Bn) of disjoint bases and every ϵ∈ℝ>0, there is a family of disjoint bases (C1,..C⌈(1−1/r−ϵ)⁢n⌉) of M so that Bi∩Cj is a singleton for all i and j.

Bucić, Kwan, Pokrovskiy and Sudakov proved the scaled weakening for r=2 [1].
