Challenge 3: Ramsey Numbers

Definition 1 (Ramsey Number).

Given t∈ℕ, the (diagonal) Ramsey number R⁢(t) is the smallest n∈ℕ∪{∞} such that every simple graph G on n vertices contains either a clique of size t or an independent set of size t.

By Ramsey’s theorem, R⁢(t) is always an integer, which is upper bounded by 4t [5, 3]. Erdős proved that random graphs G⁢(n,p) give a lower bound of the form 2t for t sufficently large [2]. An important problem that can be traced back to Ramsey’s original paper [5] is to get good upper and lower bounds for the Ramsey number.

Open Problem 1.1 (Scaled Ramsey problem).

Let r∈ℕ. Provide decimal numbers d1 and d2 with |d2−d1|≤(4−2)⋅0.9r so that for all sufficiently large t∈ℕ

d1t≤R⁢(t)≤d2t.

The scaled Ramsey problem for r=1 follows by combining the above mentioned works of Ramsey and Erdős. The problem was solved for r=2 by Campos, Griffiths, Morris and Sahasrabudhe [1] combined with the better bound from the follow-up paper by Gupta, Ndiaye, Norin and Wei [4].
