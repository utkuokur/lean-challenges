Challenge 4: Sidorenko Conjecture for Half-Graphs

Definition 1 (Graph homomorphism density).

Let G and H be simple graphs.

  1. 1.

    A function f:V⁢(H)→V⁢(G) is a graph homomorphism from H to G if for each edge u⁢v∈E⁢(H), we have f⁢(u)⁢f⁢(v)∈E⁢(G).

  2. 2.

    Let hom⁢(H,G) be the number of graph homomorphisms H→G.

  3. 3.

    The homomorphism density of H in G is the fraction

    tH⁢(G):=hom⁢(H,G)|V⁢(G)||V⁢(H)|
Conjecture 1.1 (Sidorenko’s Conjecture [1]).

For every bipartite graph H and for any graph G, we have:

tH⁢(G)≥tK2⁢(G)|E⁢(H)|

We are interested in the special case of the conjecture, where H=Hr is a half-graph with 2⁢r vertices, as follows. As far as we are aware, the conjecture has not been asked anywhere for half-graphs, and the only reason we do it for half-graphs here is that we believe that they are somewhat generic, and it will help us formulate an easier scalable version.

Definition 2 (Half-graph).

Let Ar={a1,…,ar} and Br={b1,…,br} be disjoint sets of size r. A half-graph is the bipartite graph on the vertex set Ar∪Br with the following edges present: E={ai⁢bj:i≤j} (Note that these are about ‘half’ of the possible edges between Ar and Br, hence the name).

Conjecture 1.2 (Scaled weakening of Sidorenko’s Conjecture).

Let r∈ℕ. For any graph G, we have:

tHr⁢(G)≥tK2⁢(G)(r2+r)/2
