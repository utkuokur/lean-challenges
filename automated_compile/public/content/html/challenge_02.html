Challenge 2: Excluded Minors of GF⁢(pm)-representable matroids

Definition 1 (Matroid).

A matroid is a pair M=(E,ℐ) consisting of a finite set E and a collection ℐ of subsets of E, called the independent sets, such that:

  1. 1.

    ∅∈ℐ;

  2. 2.

    if I∈ℐ and J⊆I, then J∈ℐ;

  3. 3.

    if I,J∈ℐ and |I|<|J|, then there is some x∈J∖I such that I∪{x}∈ℐ.

The maximal independent sets of M are called the bases of M.

Definition 2 (GF⁢(pm)-representable matroid).

Let p be a prime number. Let m be a positive integer. Let GF⁢(pm) denote the finite field with pm elements. A matroid M is GF⁢(pm)-representable if there is a matrix A over GF⁢(pm) such that the independent sets of M are exactly the sets of columns of A which are linearly independent over GF⁢(pm).

Definition 3 (Matroid minor).

Let M be a matroid with ground set E. The dual of M, denoted M∗, is the matroid with the same ground set, where the bases are the complements of the bases of M. Given a subset D⊆E, the deletion of D from M, denoted M∖D, is the restriction of M to E∖D. Given a subset C⊆E, the contraction of M onto E−C is the matroid M/C:=(M∗∖C)∗, with ground set E∖C.

Given matroids M,N, then N is a minor of M if there exist sets C and D such that N=M/C∖D.

Definition 4 (Excluded minor).

Let 𝒞 be a class of matroids closed under minors. A matroid M is an excluded minor for 𝒞 if M∉𝒞, but every proper minor of M belongs to 𝒞.

The class of GF⁢(pm)-representable matroids is closed under taking minors. Thus, it is natural to ask for minimal matroids which fail to be representable over GF⁢(pm).

Open Problem 1.1.

Let p be a prime number. Let m be a positive integer. List the minimal excluded minors for the class of GF⁢(pm)-representable matroids and prove that the given list is complete up to isomorphism.

For pm=2, the excluded minor list is known: a matroid is representable over GF⁢(2) if and only if it has no minor isomorphic to U2,4 [5]. For pm=3, there is also a finite explicit list: a matroid is representable over GF⁢(3) if and only if it has no minor isomorphic to U2,5, U3,5, F7, or F7∗ [1, 4]. In general, Rota’s excluded-minor conjecture, proven by Geelen, Gerards and Whittle, says that for every finite field GF⁢(pm) there are only finitely many excluded minors for the class of GF⁢(pm)-representable matroids [3, 2].

We have the following parametrized version of the open problem.

Open Problem 1.2.

Let r be a positive integer. If there is a prime number p and a positive integer m such that r=pm, then list the minimal excluded minors for the class of GF⁢(pm)-representable matroids and prove that the list is complete up to isomorphism.
